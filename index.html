Go / no-go · check in late November

All four, or the full marathon is off

  • Eating and hydrating normally for 3 straight weeks
  • One long run of 2.5 hrs or more within the last 6 weeks
  • Fresh OB clearance — late November, not September's
  • No pelvic heaviness, dragging, or leaking on any run

Decide this in advance, not in the corral. Aerobic fitness decays slowly; nutritional status doesn't. Six weeks of not keeping food down is what disqualifies a marathon, not six weeks of missed miles.

Register for the half

Most events let you drop from the full, some on race morning. If the first trimester takes your training, 13.1 at conversational pace is a reasonable thing to do off a lost block. 26.2 isn't. Pay for the option now so the decision isn't yours to make at 6am.

Stop and call your OB

  • Bleeding · fluid leakage · contractions
  • Dizziness · chest pain · headache that won't clear
  • Calf pain or swelling
  • Pelvic heaviness · leaking · pubic bone pain

Before September

  • Book a pelvic floor PT assessment.
  • Check the race's course time limit — a 6:00–7:00 finish is realistic.
